Suppose we are comparing implementations of insertion sort and merge sort on the same machine. For inputs of size n, insertion sort runs in $8n^2$ steps, while merge sort runs in 64n lg n steps. For which values of n does insertion sort beat merge sort?

Solving the inqeuality $8*n^2 > 64*n*lgn$ will tell us how large $n$ must be such that insertion sort will take longer to complete.

$$8n^2 > 64nlgn | / 8n, n \gt 0$$ $$n > 8lgn | - 8lgn$$ $$n - 8lgn > 0$$

The smallest integer for which the inequality holds is 43.
