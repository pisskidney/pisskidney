Use mathematical induction to show that when n is an exact power of 2, the solution of the recurrence $$ T(n) = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} 2 & \quad \text{if $n$ = 2}\\ 2T(n/2)+n & \quad \text{if $n = 2^k$, for k > 1} \end{array} \right. $$ is $T(n) = nlgn$

We need to prove that $T(2^n) = 2^nlg2^n$


Base case: $n = 1$

$$\begin{align}T(2^1) & = 2 =\\ &= 2^1lg2^1 \end{align}$$

The statement is true for $n = 1$


Inductive step: We assume $T(2^k) = 2^klg2^k$ holds.
$$\begin{align}T(2^{k+1}) & = 2*T({2^{k+1}\over 2}) + 2^{k+1} = \\ &= 2*T({2^k*2\over 2}) + 2^{k+1} = \\ &= 2*T(2^k) + 2^{k+1} = \\ &= 2*2^klg2^k + 2^{k+1} = \\ &= 2^{k+1}lg2^k + 2^{k+1} = \\ &= 2^{k+1}(lg2^k + 1) = \\ &= 2^{k+1}(lg2^k + lg2) = \\ &= 2^{k+1}lg2^{k+1} \end{align}$$
